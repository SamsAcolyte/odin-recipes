Olivae Conditae:

a medley of marinated olives in herbs and spices.

“There, in the midst of the city, a great olive tree stood, tall and flourishing, its branches laden with fruit. Around it twelve maidens danced gracefully, the daughters of the king.” — Homer, The Odyssy

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups green or black olives (pitted or whole, as you prefer)
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic (crushed)
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried rosemary
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • Zest of 1 lemon (or orange for a sweeter twist)
  • 1/4 cup red wine vinegar
  • Salt, to taste

Steps to Make Olivae Contidae:

  1. Prepare the Olives: Rinse the olives and place them in a bowl.
  2. Mix the Marinade: In a separate bowl, whisk together the olive oil, vinegar, garlic, thyme, rosemary, fennel seeds, peppercorns, and lemon zest.
  3. Marinate the Olives: Pour the marinade over the olives and stir to coat them evenly.
  4. Let it Sit: Cover the bowl and let the olives marinate in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours (preferably overnight) to absorb the flavors.
  5. Serve: ake the olives out and serve them at room temperature.

Relish!
